Installation Guide

Follow these simple steps to get Saros up and running in your workspace.

1

Add Saros to Slack

Start by clicking the "Add to Slack" button below. This will redirect you to Slack's authorization page.

Add to Slack
Note: You must be a workspace admin or have installation permissions to add Saros to your Slack workspace.
2

Authorize Permissions

Review the permissions Saros needs to function properly. These include:

  • Reading messages in channels and DMs
  • Posting messages in channels and DMs
  • Viewing basic information about users in your workspace

Click "Allow" to continue with the installation.

3

Connect Confluence

After adding Saros to Slack, you'll need to connect your Confluence workspace:

  1. In Slack, message Saros with the word "configure"
  2. Click the "Connect Confluence" button that appears
  3. You'll be redirected to Atlassian to authorize access
  4. Allow the requested permissions to your Confluence workspace
4

Start Using Saros

That's it! Saros will now index your Confluence content. This process may take a few minutes depending on the size of your Confluence workspace.

Once indexing is complete, you can start searching your Confluence knowledge directly in Slack:

Example queries:

  • search product roadmap
  • find onboarding documentation
  • what is our refund policy?
  • team vacation policy

You can also trigger a manual sync anytime by messaging Saros with "sync".

Troubleshooting

Saros isn't responding to my queries

  • Check if you've completed the Confluence connection
  • Try the "configure" command to verify your setup
  • Use the "sync" command to trigger a manual content sync
  • Make sure your Confluence content has been indexed (can take up to 10 minutes)

I don't see my Confluence content

  • Verify you've authorized the correct Confluence instance
  • Check that you have access to the content you're trying to search
  • Try running a manual sync by typing "sync" to Saros
